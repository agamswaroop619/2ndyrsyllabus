Objectives:

  1. To develop the ability to analyse the system while it is dynamic in nature.

  2. To develop the ability to learn the application and applicability of Newton’s law of motion.

  3. To analyse the forces and the effect of impacts of the moving bodies.

  4. To analyse the relative motion of the bodies.

  5. To examine the velocity and acceleration of the bodies in space.

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1. To develop the understanding of modeling dynamic systems of engineering using vector approach and ability to model the engineering components as particles to study their Kinematics.

CO2. Application of Newton’s laws to particles and systems of particles. Application of work energy principle, work momentum principle to particles and systems of particles. To study satellite motion using Kepler’s Law and to understand the principles of central force motion.

CO3. Modeling and analysis of forces systems acting on rigid bodies to evaluate position velocity and acceleration. To be able to draw kinematic diagrams and kinetic diagrams.

CO4. Application of Newton’s laws, Euler’s laws, Work energy principles and impulse momentum principles as rigid bodies and systems of rigid bodies.

CO5. Fundamental understanding of mechanical vibrations and finding out natural frequency for mechanical systems.

Application of free damped and forced vibrations on mechanical systems.

Unit No.

Topics to be Covered

Learning Outcomes


1.

KINEMATICS OF PARTICLES

Cartesian, cylindrical and path coordinates for velocity and acceleration. Relative motion.

Translating reference frame.

KINETICS OF PARTICLES

Equations of rectilinear and curvilinear motions in different coordinate systems. Application to system of particles.

To analyse different coordinate systems for determining the kinematics of particles.

To apply Newton’s law of motion for analyzing the forces on the moving bodies. To determine the geometrical parameters for the moving system of particles.


2.

ENERGY PRINCIPLES

Work done by a force. Potential energy. Conservative forces. Principle of conservation of energy. Power and efficiency.

IMPULSE MOMENTUM PRINCIPLE

Linear and angular momentum. Application to system of particles.

Problems involving both energy and momentum principles.

To analyse the total energy of the system of particles.

To evaluate the deformation of bodies during impact.


3.

KINEMATICS OF RIGID BODIES

Translation and rotation. Planar motion. Moving reference frame. Differentiation in a moving reference frame. Velocity and acceleration.

Angular velocity and angular acceleration.

To analyse the nature of rigid bodies motion.

To apply the kinematic principles for the

angular motion of rigid bodies.


4.

KINETICS OF RIGID BODIES

Planar motion. Translation. Rotation. General motion. Energy & momentum principles. Kinetics in 3-D motion, limited to Euler’s equations.

To understand the Euler’s equation of motion for the kinetics of rigid bodies.

To formulate the combined general plane motion, translation and rotation of rigid

bodies.


5.

MECHANICAL VIBRATIONS

Degree of freedom. Equations of motion for undamped & damped single degree systems. Vibration of rigid bodies.

CENTRAL FORCE MOTION

Two body problem. Satellite motion.

To understand the fundamentals of vibrations, their types and impacts.

To compare the motion of various satellites.

Objectives:

  1. Equip students with in-depth knowledge of cutting tool design, geometry, materials, and CNC lathe operations to enable precision in manufacturing.

  2. Develop students’ understanding and application skills in powder metallurgy, plastic moulding, gear manufacturing, and milling techniques.

  3. Provide students with expertise in finishing and super-finishing processes like grinding, honing, lapping, and high-speed machining to achieve superior surface quality.

  4. Impart comprehensive knowledge of advanced welding techniques, including GTAW, GMAW, friction stir welding, and underwater welding, for diverse industrial applications.

  5. Familiarize students with unconventional and hybrid machining methods, such as EDM, ECM, LBM, and PAM, to prepare

them for specialized manufacturing processes.

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1. A strong foundation in cutting tool design, geometry, materials, and CNC lathe operations.

CO2. Proficiency in powder metallurgy, plastic moulding methods, gear manufacturing, and milling techniques.

CO3. Expertise in finishing processes like grinding, honing, lapping, and high-speed machining for precise surface quality. CO4. Advanced knowledge of modern welding techniques, including GTAW, GMAW, friction stir, and underwater welding.

CO5. Familiarity with unconventional machining methods such as EDM, ECM, LBM, PAM, and hybrid processes for specialized applications.

Unit No.

Topics to be Covered

Learning Outcomes

BIS and other tool nomenclature systems, Geometry of a single point cutting tool, and tool materials.

Production Lathes

Milling indexing. Gear-cutting machines such as

generating, shaping, and hobbing. Gear finishing methods and template gear cutting method.

Analyse the principles of powder metallurgy, metal powders, HIP, and sintering, and evaluate the properties and applications of finished parts in industrial contexts. (Analysis)

Apply knowledge of plastic moulding methods and gear manufacturing techniques (including indexing and hobbing) to solve practical design and manufacturing

problems. (Application)


3.

Finishing and Superfinishing Processes

Operations and applications of surface, cylindrical, and centreless grinding. Reconditioning of grinding wheels. Honing, Lapping, and High-speed machining.

Explain the operations of various grinding processes (surface, cylindrical, centerless) and differentiate between them based on applications and material requirements. (Understanding)

Demonstrate the use of reconditioning techniques for grinding wheels and evaluate superfinishing processes like honing, lapping, and high-speed machining for

achieving desired surface quality. (Evaluation)


4.

Modern Welding Techniques

Atomic Hydrogen, TIG, MIG, Thermit, Friction Stir Welding, and Underwater Welding. Welding of CI, Aluminium, and Stainless Steel, Weldability, and pre- and post-treatments.

Identify various modern welding techniques such as GTAW, GMAW, friction stir, and underwater welding, and explain their applications and limitations in welding various materials. (Understanding)

Analyse pre- and post-welding treatments and justify their significance for enhancing weld quality and

material properties. (Analysis)


5.

Modern Machining Processes

Principles, process variables, and applications of ultrasonic machining (USM), electric discharge machining (EDM), electro-chemical machining (ECM), electro- chemical grinding (ECG), laser-, electron- and ion-beam machining (LBM, EBM & IBM), plasma arc machining (PAM), abrasive jet machining (AJM), and explosive

forming. Hybrid machining, and additive manufacturing processes.

Describe the principles and variables of advanced machining processes such as USM, EDM, ECM, and LBM, and compare their suitability for different material types and industrial applications. (Comprehension)

Apply knowledge of hybrid and additive manufacturing to evaluate process efficiencies and innovate solutions for complex machining challenges. (Application & Evaluation)

APPLIED THERMODYNAMICS

3

0

0

3

Objectives:

  1. To enable students to apply the principles of thermodynamics to real-world systems.

  2. To develop the ability to analyse and solve problems related to different thermodynamic processes and cycles

  3. To teach students how to design, analyse, and optimize common thermodynamic cycles such as Rankine, Brayton, Otto and Diesel.

  4. To help students understand the behavior of real gases and mixtures

  5. To foster analytical and critical thinking skills by challenging students with complex, open-ended thermodynamics problems

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1. Students will be introduced to the systems exhibiting the practical application of the laws of thermodynamics. CO2. Students will learn about construction and working of various components of energy producing systems.

CO3. Students will learn about theoretical analysis of various components of energy producing systems. CO4. Students will develop analytical problem-solving skills through numerical based problems.

CO5. Students will be able to understand the fundamental principles, design, and operation of internal combustion engines.

Unit No.

Topics to be Covered

Learning Outcomes


1.

MIXTURE OF GASES & STEAM BOILER

Dalton’s law, Dalton-Gibb’s law, Amagat-Leduc law, Thermodynamic properties of mixtures.

STEAM BOILER

Classification, Mountings and accessories. Modern High- Pressure Boilers (Lamont, Benson, Velox, Loffler). Performance of Boilers, Introduction to super critical,

fluidized bed boilers.

To understand and apply the principles of gas laws and thermodynamic properties in mixtures, analyse the classification, components, and operation of steam boilers, including high-pressure and supercritical boilers, and evaluate their performance for efficient energy conversion


2.

STEAM NOZZLES & TURBINES

Flow through nozzle, Variation of velocity, choked flow, Throat area, Design for max. Discharge, Nozzle efficiency, Off design operation of nozzle, Effect of friction on nozzle, Super saturated flow. Classification of turbines. Compounding. Velocity diagrams. Improvements in turbine plant performance. (Reheating, Regeneration, Water extraction cycle). Governing. Pass-out, Back pressure,

Mixed pressure turbines.

To understand and apply principles of fluid flow and nozzle dynamics, analyse turbine classifications and performance factors, and evaluate methods such as reheating, regeneration, and governing to improve turbine efficiency and overall plant performance in varied operational conditions


3.

CONDENSERS & COMPRESSORS

Elements of condensing plants. Jet and surface, condensers. Vacuum efficiency. Cooling towers. Different types of pumps. Reciprocating compressors. Single and multi-stage compression, Condition for minimum Work.

Efficiency of reciprocating compressors, Air motors.

To understand and apply the principles and components of condensing plants, cooling systems, pumps, and compressors, analyse the efficiency factors of condensers and reciprocating compressors, and evaluate conditions for optimal work and performance

in air compression and cooling applications.


4.

VAPOUR AND AIR STANDARD CYCLES & THERMODYNAMIC RELATIONS

Rankine cycle, Otto cycle, Diesel cycle, Dual cycle, Ericson cycle, Sterling cycle, Joule (Brayton) cycle, Comparison of these cycles with Carnot Cycle, MEP, Air standard efficiency, Maxwell relations, Joule-Thomson coefficient, Clapeyron's equation.

To understand and apply the thermodynamic principles of various engine cycles (Rankine, Otto, Diesel, etc.), analyse their performance in comparison to the Carnot cycle, and evaluate the efficiency and thermodynamic properties using concepts like MEP, air standard efficiency, and Maxwell relations


5.

I.C. ENGINES

Indicator diagram, Calculation of cylinder dimensions. Scavenging. Supercharging. Lubrication. Cooling, Governing. Performance under varying load and speed conditions. Characteristic curves. Heat balance. Brief idea of combustion problems in SI and CI engines.

To understand and apply the principles of engine performance, including indicator diagrams, cylinder dimensions, scavenging, and supercharging, analyse lubrication, cooling, and governing systems, and evaluate the effects of load and speed variations on engine performance, while considering heat balance

and combustion issues in SI and CI engines.

Objectives:

  2. To develop the ability to analyse the effect of stresses developed due to torsion, bending and shearing.

  4. To understand the fundamental approaches of fluid mechanics and types of flow.

  5. To analyse the various types of flow and their measurements.

CO1. To develop the ability to understand the underlying basic principles of mechanics of solids and fluids.

CO5. The student will be able to understand concepts of flow through pipes and open channels.

Learning Outcomes

Stress in axially loaded members, strain, constitutive laws. Axial deformation.

STRESS TRANSFORMATION

Two-dimensional stress system. Analytical and Mohr’s circle methods. Principal stresses & planes maximum shear stress.

To understand the principle of stress and strain and evaluate it for bodies under stress.

To generate the Mohr’s circle for the bodies under normal and shear

stresses.

TORSION

BENDING

To apply the flexure formula for

beams under bending stress.

COLUMN

Elastic buckling. Euler’s formula. Various end conditions. Rankine formula. Empirical formulas.

FLUID MECHANICS

Lagrangian and Eulerian approaches. Lines of flow. Path line. Streamline. Streak line. Stream tube. Types of flow. Steady and unsteady, uniform and non-uniform, laminar & turbulent. Continuity equation (one dimensional).

TYPES OF ENERGIES: Potential, Kinetic, and Pressure Energies. Bernoulli’s

To apply the Bernoulli’s theorem and related energy Principles for fluids.

FLOW MEASUREMENT

Principles. Venturimeter, horizontal, vertical & inclined. Orifices, classification, vena contracta. Hydraulic coefficients. Discharge through a large rectangular orifice.

Drowned and partially drowned orifices.

MOUTH PIECES

Types. External mouthpiece. Convergent divergent mouthpiece. Notches. Types. Rectangular and triangular notches. Weirs. Types. Rectangular weir.

Francis and basins formulae. Velocity of approach.

To compare the discharge of fluid flowing through orifice, mouthpieces and other devices.

To measure the velocity of fluid through pitot tube and other devices. To determine the coefficient of discharge, coefficient of velocity and coefficient of contraction.

FLOW THROUGH PIPES

Reynold’s experiment. Types of flow. Critical velocities. Reynold’s number. Friction loss. Darcy-Weisbach equation. Friction factor concept. Losses at entry, exit, bend and valves. Hydraulic and energy gradient lines. Power transmission through pipes. Condition for maximum power.

FLOW THROUGH OPEN CHANNELS

Chezy’s & Manning’s equations. Bazin’s and Kutter’s expressions for Chezy’s

constants. Best discharging section. Rectangular & Trapezoidal channels.

To determine the losses in flow through pipes and in open channels. To calculate the flow through various open channels.
